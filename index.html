💼 Direct Employment

Going straight into the workforce is one of the most practical and respected paths — especially when you have real skills and experience to offer. Many careers allow you to start entry-level and work your way up without any college degree.

Timeline
Immediate after graduation
Typical Entry Wage
$16–$22/hr in California
Key First Steps
Resume, references, job applications 6–8 weeks before graduation
💡 Tip: Many employers value skills and reliability over degrees. WorkAbility I can connect you to local employers before you even graduate.
🏛️ Community College

Community college is one of the best-kept secrets in education. It's affordable, close to home, flexible, and offers both career certificates and transfer pathways to 4-year universities. Every campus has a Disability Support Programs & Services (DSPS) office that provides accommodations — just like your IEP, but for college.

Cost (CA residents)
~$46/unit — some programs are free with FAFSA
Timeline to Certificate
6 months to 2 years depending on program
Key First Steps
Apply at losrios.edu, file FAFSA, contact DSPS before first semester
💡 Tip: You don't need to know your major to start. Many students take general education or exploratory classes in their first semester while deciding.
🔩 Trade / Vocational School

Trade and vocational schools give you focused, hands-on training in a specific career — usually in months, not years. Welding, HVAC, cosmetology, electrical work, medical assisting, and coding bootcamps are all examples. You graduate job-ready with a certification employers recognize.

Timeline
3 months to 2 years
Cost Range
Varies widely — DOR can often help pay
Key First Steps
Research accredited programs, apply for DOR funding, file FAFSA
💡 Tip: The California Department of Rehabilitation (DOR) can often pay for trade school tuition if you have a documented disability. Apply before you graduate.
🎓 4-Year University

A 4-year degree opens doors to professions like teaching, nursing, engineering, social work, business management, and more. California State University (CSU) campuses and the University of California (UC) system both have robust disability services offices — your accommodations follow you.

Timeline
4 years (or 2 after community college transfer)
Application Deadline
CSU/UC: Nov–Dec of senior year
Key First Steps
File FAFSA, request transcripts, ask teachers for LORs, apply by deadline
💡 Tip: Community college → transfer to CSU or UC is a well-established path that saves significant money and gives you time to figure out your major.
🎖️ Military

All branches of the military — Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Coast Guard, and National Guard — offer paid training, housing, healthcare, and education benefits (GI Bill). The military can be a structured path to a career, college funding, and lifelong skills. Talk to a recruiter early to understand your options and eligibility.

Minimum Age
17 (with parental consent) or 18
Enlistment Length
2–6 years depending on branch and role
Education Benefit
Post-9/11 GI Bill — up to 36 months of college paid
💡 Tip: The National Guard is a part-time option — you serve one weekend per month plus two weeks per year while still living and working at home. Tuition assistance is included.
🛠️ Apprenticeship / On-the-Job Training

Apprenticeships are one of the most underrated pathways — you get paid while you learn. Union apprenticeships in trades like electrical, plumbing, carpentry, and ironwork are highly structured, well-paying, and lead to lifelong careers with strong benefits. No degree required — just a commitment to show up and learn.

Starting Wage
60–80% of journeyman wage from Day 1
Timeline
3–5 years to journeyman status
Key First Steps
Research local union halls (IBEW, NECA, UA, Carpenters), attend open enrollment
💡 Tip: California's Division of Apprenticeship Standards (DAS) lists all registered programs at dir.ca.gov/das. Many programs are extremely competitive — apply to multiple.
🏕️ Job Corps

Job Corps is a free, residential program for young adults ages 16–24. You live on campus, receive job training in 100+ career areas, and earn your diploma or GED — all at no cost. Housing, meals, healthcare, and a small living stipend are provided. The Sacramento Job Corps Center is close by and serves this region.

Cost
Completely free — including housing and meals
Age Range
16–24 years old
Program Length
Typically 8 months to 2 years
💡 Tip: Apply before you graduate — there can be a waitlist. Bring your IEP and any documentation of disability if you want to request accommodations.
🔄 Gap Year / Still Deciding

Not having everything figured out is completely normal — and more common than people admit. A gap year doesn't have to mean doing nothing. It can mean working to save money, traveling, volunteering, or simply giving yourself time to figure out what you actually want. What matters is having a plan, even a loose one.

Key Priority
Stay connected to support (DOR, WorkAbility, case manager)
Risk to Avoid
Losing momentum — set a 6-month check-in goal
One Smart Move
File FAFSA anyway — it doesn't expire for a year
💡 Tip: Even if you're undecided, file FAFSA, apply to DOR, and keep your options open. Doors are much easier to walk through if you didn't let them close.

TransitionReady Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 8, 2026 · Last Updated: April 20, 2026

1. Introduction

TransitionReady is a web-based transition portfolio application designed for use by transition-age students in special education programs. TransitionReady helps students create professional resumes, cover letters, letters of recommendation requests, five-year plans, and comprehensive transition portfolios.

This Privacy Policy describes how Sure Step Education ("we," "our," or "us"), the company behind TransitionReady, collects, uses, stores, discloses, and protects student data and other personal information in connection with the TransitionReady application and website (collectively, the "Service").

We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of student information and complying with all applicable federal and state laws, including the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the California Student Online Personal Information Protection Act (SOPIPA, Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 22584), and California Education Code § 49073.1 (AB 1584).

2. Definitions

Local Educational Agency (LEA): A school district, county office of education, or charter school whose students use TransitionReady as part of a transition program.

Pupil Records: Any information directly related to a student that is maintained by the LEA or by TransitionReady on behalf of the LEA. This includes profile information, work experience, career goals, and any other personally identifiable information (PII).

Personally Identifiable Information (PII): Information that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual student's identity, either alone or when combined with other information.

Operator: Sure Step Education, doing business as TransitionReady, as a provider of an online service designed and marketed for K–12 school purposes.

Third Party / Provider: As defined in California Education Code § 49073.1 (AB 1584), the provider of digital educational software or services for the digital storage, management, and retrieval of pupil records — in this case, Sure Step Education, operating the TransitionReady application.

3. Information We Collect

TransitionReady collects only the information necessary to provide the Service. All information is entered directly by the student or generated through the student's use of the Service.

3.1 Student Information (Pupil Records)

  • Student name, contact information, and profile details
  • Profile photograph (if uploaded by the student — stored locally on the student's device only, never uploaded to our servers)
  • Education history and school information
  • Work experience and employment history
  • Career goals and five-year plan responses
  • Responses to 36 guided discovery questions covering interests, strengths, and goals
  • Selected transition program affiliations (e.g., ALTA, Job Corps, CCC, Los Rios, DOR, WorkAbility I)
  • Uploaded work samples and supporting documents
  • Generated documents including resumes, cover letters, and letters of recommendation requests

3.2 Account Information

  • Email address and name associated with the student's Google account, provided via Google OAuth sign-in
  • A unique system-generated user identifier
  • Role designation (student, case_manager, or admin)
  • Account creation and last-login timestamps

3.3 Caseload Assignment Records

When a student chooses to connect their portfolio to a case manager, we store:

  • The student's user identifier
  • The case manager's or admin's user identifier
  • The role of the staff member (case_manager or admin)
  • Connection and disconnection timestamps, and the identity of the user who made each change (for FERPA audit-trail compliance under 34 CFR § 99.32)
  • Current status of the connection (active or revoked)

3.4 Technical Information

  • Browser type and version (for responsive display purposes only)
  • No cookies, analytics trackers, or advertising pixels are used
  • Authentication tokens provided by Google OAuth and Supabase are stored in the browser solely to keep the student signed in; these tokens are not transmitted to any other party

4. How We Use Information

TransitionReady uses collected information solely for the following educational purposes:

  • To provide students with a tool for building professional transition portfolios
  • To generate career-readiness documents for use in transition planning, job applications, and post-secondary preparation
  • To enable PDF and document exports for student and school use
  • To power AI-assisted writing features that help students professionalize work experience descriptions and generate polished cover letters
  • To maintain and improve the functionality, security, and reliability of the Service

5. Prohibited Uses of Student Information

TransitionReady will NEVER:

  • Use personally identifiable information from pupil records to engage in targeted advertising directed at students, parents, or teachers
  • Use student information to create or amass a profile of a student for any purpose other than K–12 school purposes as authorized by the LEA
  • Sell, rent, or trade student personal information to any third party
  • Disclose student information to any third party except as required by law, authorized by the LEA, or as described in this Privacy Policy
  • Use student information for any commercial purpose unrelated to the provision of the Service

6. Ownership and Control of Pupil Records

In accordance with California Education Code § 49073.1 (AB 1584), all pupil records provided to or generated within TransitionReady remain the property of and under the control of the student and their Local Educational Agency (LEA). Because all student data is stored locally on the student's own device (see Section 7), students maintain direct and complete control over their information at all times.

7. Data Storage and Security

7.1 Where Data Is Stored

Student portfolio data is stored in two locations:

  • On the student's own device, via the web browser's localStorage, so the student can access their work offline and across sessions. This is the complete portfolio including profile fields, discovery responses, career goals, uploaded work sample files, and any uploaded student photograph.
  • In a Supabase-hosted cloud database operated on behalf of Sure Step Education, so that students can access their portfolio from any device and so that connected case managers can view student progress (see Section 19). The cloud copy includes text-based portfolio data, discovery responses, career goals, and completion percentages. Student photographs and uploaded work-sample files are NOT uploaded to the cloud — they remain only on the student's device. Generated document HTML is also stored only locally.

All data transmission between the student's device and the cloud database is encrypted via HTTPS/TLS.

7.2 Security Measures

  • Row Level Security (RLS): Every table in our database enforces per-user access rules at the database level. A student can only read or write their own portfolio row. A case manager can only read portfolio rows for students who have actively connected to them. Access rules are enforced by the database itself, not by application code, providing defense in depth.
  • Google OAuth authentication: We do not store or process passwords. Sign-in is handled entirely by Google. Sure Step Education never has access to account passwords.
  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit, including to/from the cloud database, the AI-assisted writing proxy, and any exports.
  • API credentials stored server-side only: Students and case managers never see or have access to API keys.
  • Staff role provisioning is manual: Case manager and administrator roles can only be assigned by a Sure Step Education database administrator. The application never auto-promotes users to staff roles. This prevents privilege escalation via self-service.
  • Audit logs: Every case manager connection and disconnection is recorded with timestamps and the identity of the user who made the change, supporting FERPA audit-trail requirements under 34 CFR § 99.32.

7.3 AI-Assisted Writing Features

TransitionReady uses the Anthropic Claude API to help students generate professional resume bullet points and cover letters. When these features are used:

  • Relevant portions of student-entered information are transmitted to Anthropic's API via a secure server-side proxy
  • Data is processed in real time and is not stored by Anthropic beyond the immediate request, under Anthropic's zero-retention commercial terms for API customers
  • Transmissions are encrypted via HTTPS and routed through a Vercel serverless function
  • A local fallback function is available if the API is unreachable
  • Student information is minimized in API payloads — only the specific fields relevant to each generation request are transmitted, not the full portfolio

Anthropic's data usage policies apply to API interactions. See anthropic.com/policies.

8. Breach Notification

TransitionReady maintains a cloud database storing text-based portfolio data as described in Section 7.1. In the event of unauthorized access, disclosure, or compromise of this data, or of any pupil records stored on our infrastructure or that of our subprocessors, TransitionReady will:

  • Notify the affected Local Educational Agency (LEA) within 72 hours of discovery
  • Describe the nature, scope, and timing of the breach
  • Identify the categories and approximate number of pupil records affected
  • Describe remediation steps taken and planned
  • Cooperate with the LEA on parent/guardian notification consistent with applicable law

Because student photographs, uploaded work-sample files, and generated documents remain only on the student's device, these categories are not at risk from a cloud-side breach. Students may reduce their own exposure by clearing browser site data when no longer needed.

9. Parental Rights: Access, Review, and Correction

In compliance with FERPA and AB 1584, parents, legal guardians, or eligible pupils (students 18+) have the right to:

  • Review information stored in TransitionReady by signing in to the student's account, or via printed/PDF exports generated from within the application
  • Correct erroneous information directly within the application at any time
  • Delete all locally-stored data by clearing the browser's site data for TransitionReady or using the application's reset function
  • Request deletion of all cloud-stored records (account and portfolio data) by contacting Sure Step Education at the address in Section 18. Deletion requests are fulfilled within 30 days. A deletion removes all records from the production database and any routine backups within the standard backup retention cycle.

10. Pupil-Generated Content

All portfolio content in TransitionReady is entered directly by the student or generated by the student through their use of AI-assisted writing features. Students retain full possession and control of their pupil-generated content at all times and may export, print, download, or delete their content without restriction. Connected case managers and administrators can view (but cannot edit) a student's portfolio in the current version of the Service.

11. Data Retention and Deletion

Portfolio data is retained as follows:

  • Local data (student photo, work sample files, generated document HTML): retained on the student's device until the student clears browser site data or uses the application's reset function.
  • Cloud data (account record, portfolio text fields, discovery responses, career goals, completion metrics, caseload assignment records): retained as long as the student's account is active. Students or their parents/guardians may request deletion at any time per Section 9. Upon deletion request, we remove all records from the production database within 30 days, and any routine backups containing the data expire according to our standard backup retention schedule (currently 30 days).
  • Audit trail records of case manager connections and disconnections: retained for the duration of the student's account plus 3 years, for FERPA audit-trail compliance under 34 CFR § 99.32. After that period, audit records are anonymized (user identifiers removed) before further retention for internal statistical purposes.
  • Inactive accounts: accounts with no sign-in activity for 3 years will receive an email notice and will be deleted 90 days later if no activity resumes.

12. FERPA Compliance

TransitionReady supports FERPA compliance by:

  • Storing portfolio data with per-user database access controls (Row Level Security) so that each student's record is accessible only to the student and to staff members actively assigned to their caseload
  • Never redisclosing personally identifiable information from pupil records to any third party except as authorized by the LEA, the eligible pupil, or as required by law
  • Maintaining audit-trail records of every staff/student connection for a minimum of 3 years past account closure, consistent with 34 CFR § 99.32
  • Honoring parent, guardian, and eligible-pupil requests to review, correct, and delete pupil records
  • Cooperating with LEAs on all FERPA-related requests and providing, on request, a list of all subprocessors with access to pupil records
  • Executing Data Processing Agreements with LEAs as described in Section 17

13. SOPIPA Compliance

As an operator of an online service designed for K–12 school purposes, TransitionReady complies with SOPIPA (Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 22584) by not engaging in targeted advertising, not amassing profiles for non-educational purposes, not selling student information, and maintaining reasonable security procedures.

14. COPPA Compliance

TransitionReady is designed for transition-age students, typically ages 16 and older. TransitionReady does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If used by students under 13, we will obtain verifiable parental consent or rely on the school consent exception under COPPA.

19. Case Manager and Administrator Access

TransitionReady allows students to share their portfolio with a designated case manager or school administrator. This section describes how that access works and what protections apply.

19.1 Student-Initiated Connection

A connection between a student's portfolio and a staff member is always initiated by the student. On the student's Profile tab, the student may enter the email address of a case manager or administrator who has a TransitionReady staff account, and click "Connect" to share access. Staff members cannot add themselves to a student's caseload.

19.2 What Staff Can See

A connected case manager or administrator has read-only access to the student's portfolio data, including:

  • Profile information (name, school, graduation year, contact info)
  • Discovery responses and guided question answers
  • Career goals and pathway selections
  • Work sample titles, descriptions, and skills (the underlying file is not uploaded to the cloud and is NOT visible to staff)
  • Completion percentage across all seven tabs
  • Last activity timestamp

19.3 What Staff Cannot Do

  • Staff cannot edit the student's portfolio in the current version of the Service. Future versions may introduce a "suggest edit" feature; any such feature will require the student to explicitly approve each suggested change before it is applied.
  • Staff cannot see or download uploaded work-sample files, uploaded supporting documents, or the student's profile photograph. These remain only on the student's device.
  • Staff cannot see a student who has not connected to them.
  • Staff cannot export or share a student's portfolio data outside the Service.

19.4 Role Provisioning

Case manager and administrator accounts are provisioned manually by Sure Step Education. The application does not automatically promote any user to a staff role. This ensures that only verified educational staff can be granted access to student portfolios.

19.5 Student Revocation

A student may disconnect from a staff member at any time via the Profile tab. Disconnection is immediate. Once disconnected, the staff member can no longer view any portion of the student's portfolio. A student may reconnect to the same staff member later if they choose.

19.6 Audit Trail

A permanent audit record of each connection and disconnection is retained, including the student's user identifier, the staff user identifier, the timestamp, and the identity of the user who performed the action. This supports FERPA audit-trail requirements under 34 CFR § 99.32. Audit records are retained for 3 years after account closure (see Section 11).

19.7 Staff Obligations

Case managers and administrators with access to a student's portfolio are bound by the confidentiality and use restrictions described throughout this Privacy Policy. Staff may not disclose student information to any third party except as authorized by the student, by the student's parent or legal guardian (for students under 18), by the student's LEA, or as required by law. Staff access to pupil records constitutes access to educational records under FERPA and is subject to the same protections.

19.8 Technical Enforcement

All access controls described in this section are enforced at the database level via Row Level Security policies. Access decisions are not left to client-side code. A staff member whose assignment has been revoked cannot access the student's portfolio even if they still have an active application session.
